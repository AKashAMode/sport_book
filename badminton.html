BADMINTON


Originate: Badminton has roots tracing back to ancient civilizations but gained its modern form in the mid-19th century in British India.

Player: It is played by either two opposing players (singles) or two opposing pairs (doubles)

Ground: rectangular court divided by a net, measuring 13.4 meters long and 6.1 meters wide for doubles.

Players use racquets to hit a shuttlecock over the net, aiming to land it within the opponent's court. Badminton is known for its fast-paced rallies, requiring agility, precision, and strategic planning, and it features prominently in international competitions like the Olympics.
